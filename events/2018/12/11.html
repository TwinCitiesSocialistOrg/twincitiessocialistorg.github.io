A World Without Borders

Tuesday, December 11th
6:30pm - 8pm

Room 180C - Wilson Library
West Bank Campus - University of Minnesota,
19th Ave S, Minneapolis, MN

Summary

We'll be having a political discussion on why international solidarity is so important for the working class and what that looks like. We'll also be reflecting on end of semester events and the public forum on immigrant detention we did with the No Cages Minnesota coalition.

Recomended reading

The case against “The case against open borders”

  • Justin Akers Chacón, author of the recently published Radicals in the Barrio, a rich history of the Mexican and Chicano working class in the U.S., provides a response that sets the record straight for the left on the question of open borders.
The dead end of "Comprehensive Immigration Reform"
  • Lucy Herschel argues why it’s time for the immigrant justice movement to reject the familiar three-word solution that’s been touted by Democrats for the past 10 years.
Madison rallies for migrant rights
  • Summer Coff reports from Madison, Wisconsin, on a demonstration against the U.S. government’s cruel treatment of migrants and refugees.
